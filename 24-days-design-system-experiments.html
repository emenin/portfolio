A compact write-up of 24 tiny prompts, experiments, and lessons from a year of design system work.

Why this experiment

I love design systems, and I love the coziness of the Christmas season. So I created an Advent-style experiment with 24 prompts and ideas for system builders.

It was a way to play with Figma Make more intentionally and keep the work light, curious, and practical.

What it is

  • A daily prompt inspired by systems work, research, and design patterns.
  • A mix of tiny experiments, thought starters, and practical exercises.
  • A low-pressure way to stay inspired while learning or working on system habits.

How it works

Each day unlocks a new insight or activity. Some are things I tried, some live in my ideas bank, and some emerged while building the experiment.

The goal was simple: give design system people a small gift that sparks new thinking, without demanding a big project.

The outcome

The calendar became a playful way to keep systems work front of mind, and a reminder that experimentation can happen through tiny, regular actions.

If you try one of the prompts, I’d love to know how it goes.

Takeaways

  • Small experiment routines help make systems thinking more approachable.
  • Design systems benefit from curiosity, not only from big strategy work.
  • A lightweight structure can keep a habit engaging throughout the season.
